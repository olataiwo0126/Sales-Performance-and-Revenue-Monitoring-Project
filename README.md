# Creating a Dashboard using Power BI to Monitor Sales Performance of a Company

### project overview

As part of my data analytics journey, I developed an end-to-end sales performance dashboard using Microsoft Power BI, demonstrating my ability to combine technical data skills with business insight generation.
This project analyses transactional data from a boutique furniture and household equipment store, with the objective of transforming raw data into actionable intelligence that supports strategic decision-making.

### Data sources

The dataset, sourced from Kaggle, includes key variables such as Order ID, Revenue (Amount), Profit, Quantity, Category, Sub-category, Payment Mode, and Unit Cost.

### Technical Approach and Methodology

I applied a structured data analysis workflow, showcasing core data analytics competencies:
- Data Cleaning & Preparation:
   - Identified and handled missing values, duplicates, and inconsistencies to ensure data quality and reliability. 
- Feature Engineering:
   - Created new calculated fields such as Profit Status, Profit Margin, and Order Performance Metrics to enable deeper analysis. 
- DAX Measures Development:
   - Built dynamic measures including Total Revenue, Total Profit, Total Loss, Total Orders, Maximum/Minimum Values, and Total Quantity to support KPI tracking

### Business Problem

The goal of this analysis was to answer critical business questions that impact profitability and operational efficiency, including:
- What is the total profit generated? 
- Which sub-categories contribute most to profit? 
-	How are orders distributed across product categories? 
-	Which payment methods drive the highest transactions? 
-	How does revenue perform across categories in terms of profit and loss?

### Dashboard Design

Designed a wireframe in Microsoft PowerPoint and translated it into a fully interactive Power BI dashboard, emphasizing clarity, usability, and business storytelling through: 
-	KPI cards 
-	Trend analysis (sales and orders) 
-	Category and sub-category performance 
-	Payment mode insights

### Key Insights

- The Clothing category emerged as the top revenue and profit driver. 
- The Electronics category recorded low sales, resulting in losses. 
-	Cash on Delivery (COD) is the most preferred payment method among customers. 

### Business Recommendations

-	Optimize inventory by increasing stock in high-performing categories like Clothing. 
-	Implement targeted marketing strategies to improve underperforming categories such as Electronics. 
-	Prioritize and expand high-margin product lines. 
-	Enhance customer experience around COD while promoting alternative digital payment options.
